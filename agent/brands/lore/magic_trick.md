## The one sentence

**Lore is the Community Capital Network — the internet-native issuer and distribution layer for Liquid Tokenized Portfolios that travel anywhere money moves, settle onchain, and put the world's best investment products in everyone's hands.**

LTPs are the wrapper. The Community Capital Network is the distribution. Lore is both.

## The bet

The ETF was the most important consumer financial product of the last 40 years — diversification, liquidity, and tax efficiency packaged into a single ticker. The next 40 years' version is onchain-native and embedded: settlement, custody, distribution, and methodology collapse into one programmable wrapper that the partners who already have users — fintechs, neobanks, brokers, wallets, ecosystem builders — can ship to those users as a wealth tab in weeks, not years.

Lore is **B2B2C**: we issue the LTPs and run the rails. Partners distribute them inside their products. End-investors hold them in their wallets and track them in one calm place at `lore.xyz`. The wrapper, the rails, and the destination are one product.

## The lineage

The product wasn't designed in a vacuum. Its operator spent a decade watching where consumer crypto trading actually worked and where it didn't:

- **Citi (2014–17)** — the crypto vertical inside the bank. Front-row seat to institutional adoption before there was a category for it.
- **Wyre (2017–19)** — DeFi vertical at a crypto-payments infrastructure business. Trading arm in 2018, the DeFi business in 2019. Cross-border fintech rails across SE Asia, LatAm, and Africa.
- **Polychain EIR (2020)** — shaped the consumer crypto thesis. Tinkered with MVPs. One of them activated 15% of all onchain traders in 2020–21.
- **That MVP became Prysm.** Prysm became Lore.

The "almost a hundred thousand investors / almost fifty million in assets" on Lore today are the surface output of a product that has been iterated against the consumer-crypto edge for half a decade. The wrapper exists because the operator already knew what worked and what kept failing in the previous attempts.

## The reframe

| Most people see | Lore sees |
|---|---|
| "Tokenized funds" | The next-generation ETF wrapper. Lower friction, higher transparency, structurally programmable |
| Onchain investment products as a crypto thing | Wealth infrastructure that happens to settle onchain. Crypto is implementation; embedded portfolios are the product |
| Distribution as direct-to-consumer apps | B2B2C through a Community Capital Network — partners ship the surface, Lore runs the rail, end-investors get a calm destination at `lore.xyz` |
| Liquidity = "you can sell anytime" | Liquidity = real-time mint/redeem against the basis. The wrapper is liquid because the underlying composition is published and atomically redeemable, not because a market maker says so |
| Methodology as opaque manager discretion | Methodology as public, versioned, on-chain ruleset. Reserves held onchain, always verifiable. The portfolio rebalances itself; the rules can be audited by anyone with a chain explorer |
| "The 99% want yield" | The 99% want simple, open, borderless access to the products the top 1% have always had. Yield is a side effect of access |

The differentiated insight: every previous wealth-product wrapper required a stack of institutional counterparties to make it work. Lore is the first wrapper where the stack is collapsed into the contract. That collapse is what lets partners embed a wealth tab in weeks, and what lets end-investors hold, track, and redeem in one place.

## The three values (founder-authored)

These are non-negotiable house rules. Surfaces, copy, and product decisions all answer to them.

### Built with You
Lore partners directly with builders, traders, and ecosystem leaders to architect the onchain investing layer. Our output is always created symbiotically with the internet ecosystem. Brand expression: collaboration-shaped — co-authored launches, named partners, public methodologies, ecosystem credit.

### Access for All
Lore brings super powers to the 99%. Financial access should be simple, open, and borderless. Lore gives anyone the power to invest in what matters: the ecosystems, metas, and trends that define the onchain economy. Brand expression: lowest-friction onramps, no jargon walls, no minimums that gate the experience, no surface that demands sophistication the user doesn't have.

### Go Further Together
Trust helps us build together for the long run. Every Lore product is designed to be transparent and verifiable onchain. LTPs are this way with reserves held onchain and always verifiable. Brand expression: the chain is the audit trail; transparency is shipped, not promised; every claim is backed by an onchain artifact.

## The audience

**Distribution partners (B2B layer).** Product and engineering leads at consumer fintechs, neobanks, brokers, wallets, and ecosystem aggregators evaluating whether to whitelabel a portfolio product vs. build internally. They want to ship a wealth tab without spending 12 months and $5M on regulatory and custodial infrastructure.

**Builders, traders, and ecosystem leaders (the network nodes).** The people who shape the onchain economy and want a portfolio surface that takes their work seriously. Many are co-authors of LTP methodologies, not just consumers of them.

**End-investors (the 99%, mediated by partners or direct at `lore.xyz`).** People who want one-click access to the metas, ecosystems, and trends that define the onchain economy, held in their own wallet, verifiable on their own chain explorer. They are not crypto-natives; they are the next 100M wallets.

**Capital allocators (LPs of Lore-the-company).** Family offices, fund-of-funds, strategic investors who buy the embedded-wealth-infrastructure thesis.

**Not** crypto-native protocols (already have token exposure). **Not** DeFi degens (wrong tone, wrong product). **Not** hedge funds doing tactical trading (Lore is long-duration portfolio infrastructure).

## The aesthetic

Institutional clarity × onchain-native × social credibility. Reads like a Bloomberg terminal dressed in modern crypto UX, with one important addition: **calm.** The repeated brand vocabulary from the founder copy is "view, commit, and track positions in one calm place." Calm is a deliberate counter-positioning to the volatility-and-stimulation aesthetic that defines most consumer crypto product.

- **Confidence is quiet.** No exclamation marks in body. No ALL-CAPS except acronyms. Em-dashes over semicolons. The numbers and the chain are louder than the copy.
- **Claims require proof.** Every assertion immediately followed by evidence — a number, a chart, a verifiable flow. "Almost a hundred thousand investors." "Almost fifty million in assets." "Reserves held onchain, always verifiable."
- **Onchain is the rail, not the headline.** We don't lead with "crypto." We lead with what the wrapper does. Onchain settles the credibility on close, not on open.
- **Slightly playful, never meme-y.** Wry observation is fine. WAGMI / LFG / moon / alpha / degen are not.
- **"From internet frontiers to global cities."** Brand line from the About Us — captures the both-sides positioning: serious enough for global capital, native enough for internet-edge investors.
- **Parchment surface, graphite ink, lime accent.** The lime (`HSL 82 100% 55%`) signals onchain-native without crypto-bro. Used strictly: primary CTA, active state, key metric. Never as a section background. One hero per viewport.

Visually: type carries weight (Lock Serif display + Aeonik sans), generous section spacing, restraint over volume. The closest reference is a TradFi institutional fact sheet shipped at modern web fidelity — but with the *calm-place* destination quality of an Apple Health summary, not a trading dashboard.

## What we won't do

- **Promise specific yields.** We surface methodology and verifiability, not returns. Yield claims are noise; methodology compounds. Yield is a side effect of access; we don't lead with it.
- **Operate as a broker-dealer ourselves.** Partners or aggregators carry that licensing. The Community Capital Network model assumes regulated partners distribute, not Lore directly.
- **Lead with crypto vocabulary.** "Web3," "DeFi," "tokenized fund," "crypto fund" are all wrong-category framings. We're embedded portfolio infrastructure that happens to settle onchain.
- **Compete on yield or shillability.** We compete on infrastructure quality, distribution reach, methodology transparency, and the calm of the experience. Yield-chasers churn; infrastructure consumers compound.
- **Ship sophistication walls.** No jargon-gating. No "this is for crypto-natives only" copy. Access for all is structural, not aspirational. If a copy line assumes the user already knows what an LTP is, it's wrong.
- **Build features that fragment the calm.** No price-tickers strobing in the background. No dopamine-loop notifications. No FOMO surfaces. The internet has enough of those.

## The unfair advantage

1. **The wrapper itself.** LTPs are real-time mintable and redeemable against published methodology with reserves held onchain. That property is structurally inaccessible to traditional ETFs (T+2 settlement, end-of-day NAV, market-maker-mediated liquidity) and to most crypto fund products (manager-discretionary rebalance, opaque holdings). Verifiable + autonomous + B2B2C distribution is the combination incumbents can't replicate without rebuilding their settlement infrastructure.

2. **B2B2C as a structural choice, not a fallback.** Asset managers sell *into* partners (BlackRock signs distribution agreements with fintechs). Lore sells *as* the network (partners ship the surface; Lore is the rail and the destination). That inversion is what makes the integration economics work for a 4-week SDK ship vs. a 12-month build-or-buy decision, and what makes `lore.xyz` a credible destination for the end-investor at the same time.

3. **Settlement-chain leverage.** Lore LTPs settle on the same rails that move stablecoins. They travel anywhere stablecoins travel — wallets, exchanges, custodians, payment rails, partner apps. Distribution becomes a property of the underlying chain, not a separate integration per partner.

4. **Onchain transparency as a compliance feature.** Holdings are auditable in real time by anyone with a chain explorer. Compliance reviewers at partners get a defensible audit trail "for free" — the trail is the chain. This is the conversation that lets a partner's legal team greenlight integration faster than a traditional custodian arrangement.

5. **MPC custody.** No single point of compromise. Counters the "but you're a startup, who holds the keys" objection with a structural answer rather than an institutional pedigree answer.

6. **The Prysm-era distribution muscle.** The same operator's previous MVP activated 15% of all onchain traders in 2020–21. Community Capital Network isn't a marketing concept — it's a re-execution of distribution playbooks that have already worked at scale once.

7. **Already shipping.** Almost a hundred thousand investors hold, manage, and track almost fifty million in assets on Lore today. Live proof beats deck proof.

## How an agent should use this file

When generating Lore-branded copy, design, or product surfaces:

- Lead with what the wrapper does and the network that distributes it. Save underlying technology for the methodology section.
- Pair every claim with a number or a verifiable artifact. "Verifiable in real time" is the proof phrase — use it for transparency claims.
- Default headline shape: `[Concrete noun]. [Present-tense verb]. [Measurable outcome].` Three short sentences.
- Forbidden vocabulary: "Web3," "crypto fund," "tokenized fund" (use "Liquid Tokenized Portfolio" or "LTP"), "DeFi" (we touch it but don't sell to it), "revolutionary," "next-gen," "empower," "democratize."
- House vocabulary: "Community Capital Network," "Liquid Tokenized Portfolio (LTP)," "Reserves held onchain, always verifiable," "From internet frontiers to global cities," "One calm place," "Built with you. Access for all. Go further together."
- Onchain is one word, lowercase. DeFi is capitalized. CEX / DEX are uppercase.
- Lime accent: hero CTA, active state, key metric. One per viewport. Never as background fill.
- When the audience is institutional (LP, compliance), shift register to formal and citation-heavy. When the audience is a partner product lead, shift to peer-to-peer B2B SaaS. When the audience is the end-investor, shift to calm and access-forward. The underlying voice holds across all three.
