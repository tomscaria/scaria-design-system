# Positioning

> Agent-readable positioning. Derived from the existing brand voice doc + audience interviews. Founder-confirm before treating as final.

## The category

**Lore is infrastructure for putting traditional and onchain finance in one wrapper.** Specifically: Liquid Tokenized Portfolios (LTPs) — onchain ETFs that bundle crypto assets + tokenized equities + savings rails into a single Portfolio Token. Distribution is white-label; the product surface is the partner's, not ours.

## Where we live in the market

Between three things and not on top of any of them:

- **Traditional asset managers** (BlackRock, Vanguard) — they understand the wrapper, don't have the onchain rails.
- **Crypto-native funds** (Bitwise, Galaxy) — onchain rails, but indexed and CEX-distributed, not white-label-embeddable.
- **Embedded finance** (Mercury, Treasury Prime) — embeddable, but no portfolio / investment product layer.

Lore is the **portfolio layer for embedded finance**, with onchain rails as the unfair advantage (verifiable, programmable, settlement-agnostic).

## Who we sell to

**Distribution partners** building wealth tabs into existing products. Initial focus is on partners with:
- A consumer base that wants exposure to onchain assets but not crypto custody complexity
- Regulatory cover for offering investment products (or partnership w/ a broker-dealer)
- A reason to whitelabel rather than build (speed, capital efficiency)

**Not:** retail end-users (we don't ship a B2C app). Not: crypto-native protocols (they already have token exposure).

## The wedge

Our LTP wrapper is **verifiable in real time** (you can audit holdings on-chain at any moment) and **rebalances autonomously** (no manual portfolio management). That combination — verifiable + autonomous — is what we have that incumbents structurally can't replicate without rebuilding their settlement infrastructure.

## What we won't do

- Ship a B2C wallet / consumer app under the Lore brand. Distribution is partner-led.
- Promise specific yields. We surface methodology and verifiability, not returns.
- Market to retail investors directly.
- Operate as a broker-dealer ourselves. Partners or aggregators carry that licensing.

## Headline patterns

Per `voice.md`:

- "One portfolio. Fully onchain. Verifiable in real time."
- "Three products. One account. Zero custody risk."
- "The portfolio rebalances itself. Monthly. Onchain."

## Reframes we use

- "Crypto" → "onchain assets" (de-culturalize)
- "Investment platform" → "portfolio infrastructure" (sell the rail, not the product)
- "Tokenized" → "Liquid Tokenized" (the liquidity is the differentiator)
- "Web3 fund" → just "fund" (the web3-ness is implementation; don't make it the headline)
