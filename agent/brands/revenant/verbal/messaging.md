# revenant — messaging

## Hero hierarchy (for rswarm.ai)

### Level 1 — H1 candidates

**Preferred:**
> A research lab. Ships tools.

**Alternates:**
> Run the loop a fund runs.
>
> The apparatus, not the alpha.
>
> Systematic infrastructure for working quants.

**Banned for H1:**
- Anything starting with "The future of..."
- Anything containing "AI-powered"
- Anything with the word "democratize"

### Level 2 — sub-hero

**Preferred:**
> Model fit → CMA-ES sweep → paper → live. Birth, Canary, Apex, Revenant. The lifecycle is the product.

Key constraint: the sub-hero **names the lifecycle stages**. The vocabulary is part of the offer.

### Level 3 — credibility bar (above the fold)

Three concrete artifacts. Each is a live link:
- Latest calibration plot (auto-updated)
- Current lifecycle dashboard (counts at each stage)
- This month's Bench (and why)

Not logos. Not press quotes. Artifacts.

## Section-level messaging

### "What the tool does"

**Lead with the loop, not the features.**

> Define a regime. Fit a model. Sweep the parameter space with CMA-ES at a sane cost. Backtest with honest fees. Promote to Canary. Watch the calibration. If it holds, Apex. If it dies, Bench.

### "Who it's for"

**Use the reference cohort directly. Don't sanitize.**

> If you've been running systematic strategies on your own capital and the part that breaks isn't your alpha but your infrastructure — this is for you. If you spend Saturdays in a quant Discord arguing about regime detection — this is for you.

### "What it isn't"

**This section earns trust by closing exits.**

> Not a fund. Not signals. Not a managed service. No sales calls. The lab trades its own strategies; that's evidence, not the offer.

### "How we charge"

**Be specific. Quant readers respect price clarity.**

> [TBD with founder — price model placeholder. Likely: free tier with sweep budget cap, paid tier with higher cap + priority queue, no annual contracts.]

## Email / Discord / drip — voice continuity

Same voice, lower altitude. Drop the H1 framings; lead with the artifact.

**Subject line patterns that work:**
- "Calibration update: [strategy id]"
- "Moved to Bench: [strategy id] — here's why"
- "Canary on a new regime classifier"
- "CMA-ES sweep cost analysis (Q[X])"

**Subject line patterns that don't:**
- "Big news!"
- "You won't believe..."
- "Your alpha awaits"
- Anything with an emoji

## Long-form (research notes)

Long-form is where the brand is most itself. Conventions:
- Always include the failure case.
- Show fees + slippage assumptions explicitly.
- Name the optimizer. Name the venue. Name the lookback.
- Plots are first-class; prose explains the plot, not the other way around.
- Sign off with a question, not a CTA.
