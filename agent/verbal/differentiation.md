# Differentiation

> What separates Lore from the alternatives a partner / allocator might consider. Founder-confirm.

## vs traditional asset managers entering onchain (BlackRock BUIDL, Franklin Templeton, etc.)

| Dimension | Them | Lore |
|---|---|---|
| Surface ownership | Their brand on their product | White-label; partner owns the surface |
| Onchain integration depth | Tokenized share class wrapper | Native onchain settlement + verifiability |
| Speed to ship for a partner | 12+ months | 4 weeks SDK integration (claimed) |
| Pricing model | Fee on AUM | Fee on AUM + partner economics |
| Distribution model | Direct + advised | Whitelabel-first |

**Key wedge:** they sell *into* partners; we sell *as* partners.

## vs crypto-native funds (Bitwise, Galaxy, 21Shares)

| Dimension | Them | Lore |
|---|---|---|
| Product format | ETF / closed-end fund | Liquid Tokenized Portfolio (LTP) |
| Distribution | CEX listing, broker channels | Whitelabel into partner apps |
| Custody | Qualified custodian | MPC + onchain settlement |
| Rebalance | Manager-discretionary | Methodology-driven, autonomous |

**Key wedge:** their distribution is broker-mediated; ours is embedded.

## vs embedded finance providers (Mercury, Treasury Prime, Stripe Treasury)

| Dimension | Them | Lore |
|---|---|---|
| Product depth | Banking + cards | Portfolio + investments |
| Asset class | USD + cash equivalents | Diversified, including onchain exposure |
| Settlement | Traditional rails | Onchain |

**Key wedge:** they end at the savings tab; we extend into the invest tab.

## vs DIY (partner builds in-house)

| Dimension | DIY | Lore |
|---|---|---|
| Time | 12-24 months engineering | 4 weeks integration |
| Capital | $5M+ to stand up infrastructure | SaaS fee structure |
| Regulatory | Partner takes full burden | Lore + sub-licensed partners share |
| Onchain settlement | Has to be built or acquired | Built |

**Key wedge:** make-vs-buy math. We're the buy.

## What we lose on (be honest)

- **Brand recognition** vs BlackRock — partners' legal teams reach for incumbents first.
- **Track record** vs Bitwise — they have years; we have months.
- **Surface area** vs Mercury — they have orders of magnitude more customer touchpoints.

How we counter:
- BlackRock can't be embedded — we can.
- Bitwise is CEX-listed — we're embeddable.
- Mercury doesn't do portfolio — we do.

Each competitor wins on one axis; nobody wins on the axis we own (embedded + onchain + portfolio).
