## Brand voice

**Institutional clarity × onchain-native × social credibility.**

Lore reads like a Bloomberg terminal dressed in modern crypto UX.
Every sentence should feel like it was written by someone who manages money.
Not someone who tweets about it.

---

## Tone spectrum

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Hero headline | Confident, declarative | "One portfolio. Fully onchain. Verifiable in real time." |
| Product description | Precise, calm | "Rebalanced monthly across six asset classes." |
| Data callout | Factual, understated | "$42M AUM across 1,200 wallets." |
| CTA | Direct, slightly playful | "Start watching." / "See the portfolio." |
| Error / empty state | Honest, helpful | "Nothing here yet. Connect a wallet to begin." |

---

## Principles

### 1. Claims require proof
Every assertion must be immediately followed by evidence: a number, a chart, a verifiable flow.

**Bad:** "We're the best onchain portfolio platform."
**Good:** "12-month CAGR of 34 %. Verifiable on Etherscan."

### 2. Short sentences win
Target ≤ 15 words per sentence. If a sentence needs a comma, consider splitting it.

### 3. Declarative > imperative
State what the product does, not what the user should feel.

**Bad:** "Imagine a world where your portfolio manages itself."
**Good:** "The portfolio rebalances itself. Monthly. Onchain."

### 4. Calm > loud
Confidence is quiet. Hype is loud. We are confident.

### 5. Slightly playful — never meme-y
A wry observation is fine. Internet slang, emojis in copy, or crypto in-jokes are not.

## Banned words & phrases

These terms are **never** acceptable in any Lore output:

| Category | Banned | Replacement heuristic |
|---|---|---|
| Hype | revolutionary, disruptive, game-changing, next-gen | State the concrete improvement |
| Vague empowerment | empower, unlock potential, democratize | Describe the specific capability |
| Crypto clichés | WAGMI, LFG, moon, alpha, degen | Use standard financial language |
| Filler | leverage (as verb), synergy, ecosystem play | Cut the word; rewrite the sentence |
| Futurism | reimagine, paradigm shift, web3 revolution | Describe what exists today |

## Punctuation & formatting

- **No exclamation marks** in body copy. One per page maximum, and only in a CTA.
- **No ALL-CAPS** words except acronyms (AUM, TVL, CEX).
- **No em-dashes** (—). Em-dashes read as AI-generated. Replace by job: pause/pivot → period + fragment; bridging two ideas → period; aside/clarification → commas or parentheses; coda or list at end → period; second clause expands first → colon.
- **No semicolons** unless joining two genuinely paired short clauses.
- **Oxford comma** always.
- **Numbers:** Use digits for all values ≥ 2. Spell out "one" and "zero."

---

## AI fingerprint anti-patterns (strip aggressively)

These constructions signal AI-generated copy and must be avoided:

- **"It's not just X, it's Y"** rhetorical structure. Replace with a single declarative claim.
- **Aggressive parallel triplets.** Mix in pairs and quads to break the AI rhythm.
- **"X is the new Y" framing.** Stale.
- **Adjective stacking** ("dynamic, results-driven, passionate"). Cut all.
- **Self-describing meta-commentary** ("We communicate clearly", "We're detail-oriented").

## Headline formula

```
[Concrete noun] + [present-tense verb] + [measurable outcome].
```

Examples:
- "A single portfolio. Rebalanced monthly. Verifiable in real time."
- "Social credibility, scored onchain."
- "Three products. One account. Zero custody risk."
