## Why the apparatus matters — the gold-rush analogy

In 1849, mining gold meant stumbling onto a nugget. The math of the gold rush was: be there first, be there at all, get lucky. The winners were a small population of people in the right place at the right time.

Today, more gold is mined every year than at any point in human history. Not because the deposits got richer — they got *much* poorer. Modern mines extract gold from low-grade ore at parts-per-million concentrations. A modern operation processes tens of thousands of tons of rock to recover a few hundred ounces. The math inverted: yield is no longer a function of finding a nugget; yield is a function of the apparatus you've built to sift volume.

The same shift has happened to financial edge. The era of privileged access — being early to a tip, being in the room when a deal got done, knowing the analyst at the prime broker — is closing. Information is more public and more abundant than it has ever been. Per-edge concentration has collapsed. There are no more giant gold nuggets to stumble on.

But the total amount of *extractable* edge in the system is higher than it has ever been. Onchain venues publish every trade. Prediction markets crowdsource probabilities in real time. Tokenized assets create observable spreads across venues. Methodology can be published, versioned, audited. **The ore is richer than ever, and it's available to anyone — if they have the apparatus.**

The next forty years of finance will be won by the people who build the best sifting apparatus, not the people with the best access. Scaria, Inc. builds the apparatus for two substrates simultaneously.

## What we build

Two products. Two substrates. Same thesis.

The Community Capital Network. Liquid Tokenized Portfolios that travel anywhere money moves, distributed B2B2C through fintechs, neobanks, brokers, and ecosystem partners, with a calm-place destination at `lore.xyz` where the end-investor can view, commit, and track positions. Almost a hundred thousand investors. Almost fifty million in assets. Reserves held onchain, always verifiable.

What Lore is sifting: the methodology layer that used to require a Bloomberg fee and a relationship with an asset manager. The ore is public asset prices, public methodologies, and public partner distribution. The apparatus is the LTP wrapper plus the B2B2C rail.

The edges-for-all expression: a partner app — a neobank, a wallet, a broker — can ship a wealth tab in 4 weeks that gives its users institutional-grade diversified onchain exposure with verifiable holdings. The 99% get access to the products the top 1% have always had.

### Revenant — collective intelligence for agents

A research lab studying how autonomous agent systems behave, evolve, and fail when real capital is at stake, plus a tools business for the self-service quants who've been complaining about abandoned venue APIs in Polymarket and Kalshi Discords for 24+ months.

The brand-facing surface is a population of trading agents that earn their way to live capital through a pre-registered lifecycle — Birth → Canary → Apex → Revenant — with numeric promotion gates and committed falsification floors. Cross-LLM-family judging (Opus + GPT-5 + Gemini). Population-level Darwinian selection across runs. Death is data; graveyard entries train the next generation.

What Revenant is sifting: the firehose of every paper, every venue, every signal. The ore is public market microstructure and the entire arXiv corpus on agentic finance. The apparatus is the swarm plus the tools that let the cohort verify it themselves.

The edges-for-all expression: a methodology that previously required Renaissance or Two Sigma scale — systematic edge extraction with continuous selection — running at solo-operator infrastructure cost, with the tooling shipped publicly so the self-service quant cohort can compound their own books with it.

## Why the same roof — softer crossover

Lore and Revenant share a founder, a substrate, and a thesis. They do not share systems.

- **Same founder.** A decade of operating against the same migration produces the conviction to express it twice. Without the operator background, neither product gets the operational shape it has — the Wyre cross-border experience shows up in Revenant as the fragmentation playbook and in Lore as the B2B2C distribution muscle.
- **Same substrate.** Both run on onchain rails. Both depend on the same operational competencies — settlement, MPC custody, cross-venue execution, regional regulatory awareness. The substrate is the moat shared across products.
- **Same thesis.** Both replace privileged-access alpha with apparatus-driven alpha. Lore aggregates human preference and capital. Revenant aggregates agent processing and selection. The collective-intelligence framing is the same on both sides.

They do **not** share methodology pipelines, codebases, or strategy fleets. Crossover happens through the operator and the substrate — not through the systems themselves. Either product alone is a fund or a startup. Together, they're a conviction expressed twice.

## What we won't do

Shared boundaries across the house:

- **No "giant gold nugget" framing.** No "get rich quick." No "the next 100x." We don't celebrate stumbled-upon edge because that's not the math anymore.
- **No promising returns.** We promise methodology and apparatus. Numbers belong in the report, not the pitch.
- **No retail manipulation.** Lore distributes B2B2C through regulated partners and a calm direct destination. Revenant sells tools to a cohort that knows what they're buying. Neither product chases attention or runs FOMO surfaces.
- **No hype vocabulary.** "Revolutionary," "disruptive," "game-changing," "democratize," "empower," "next-gen," "Web3 revolution" — these are nugget-era words. The apparatus era uses falsifiable claims and audit trails.
- **No closed-source apparatus where it can be open.** Methodology is public and versioned. The chain is the audit trail. Papers are on SSRN. Gates are pre-registered. Reserves are verifiable.
- **No discretionary anything.** Discretion is the privileged-access era's leftover. The apparatus, not the operator, makes the call.
